## Section II: Free Response

### Part A — Calculator Allowed (2 questions · 30 minutes)

---

**1.** Ganga is training for her driving test by practicing on a straight stretch of road near Torrey Pines. Her velocity, in feet per second, is modeled by the piecewise function:

$$v(t) = \begin{cases} 6t - t^2 & 0 \le t \le 4 \\ 8\sin\!\left(\frac{\pi(t-4)}{6}\right) & 4 < t \le 10 \end{cases}$$

where $t$ is measured in seconds.

**(a)** Find Ganga's acceleration at $t = 3$ seconds. Is her speed increasing or decreasing at that moment? Justify your answer.

**(b)** Find the total distance Ganga travels from $t = 0$ to $t = 10$ seconds.

**(c)** At what time $t$ in the interval $[4, 10]$ does Ganga's velocity first equal $4$ ft/s? Find her acceleration at that instant.

**(d)** Is there a time $t$ in the interval $[0, 4]$ at which Ganga's acceleration is $0$? Justify your answer using a theorem from calculus.

---

**2.** The number of visitors to Balboa Park on a summer day can be modeled by the rate function

$$R(t) = 200e^{-0.1(t-14)^2}$$

where $R(t)$ is measured in visitors per hour and $t$ is the number of hours after midnight, for $8 \le t \le 22$.

**(a)** How many total visitors enter the park between 8:00 AM ($t = 8$) and 10:00 PM ($t = 22$)? Round to the nearest whole number.

**(b)** Find the average rate at which visitors enter the park from $t = 8$ to $t = 22$. Indicate units.

**(c)** At what time $t$ does the rate of visitors entering the park reach its maximum? Justify your answer.

**(d)** Visitors leave the park at a rate modeled by $L(t) = 15(t - 8)$ visitors per hour for $8 \le t \le 22$. At what time $t$ is the number of visitors in the park the greatest? Justify your answer.

### Part B — No Calculator (4 questions · 60 minutes)

**3.** Consider the differential equation $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x + 1}{y}$, where $y \ne 0$.

**(a)** On the axes provided (sketch your own), draw a slope field for the given differential equation at the twelve points indicated: $(x, y)$ for $x \in \{-1, 0, 1\}$ and $y \in \{-2, -1, 1, 2\}$.

**(b)** Find the particular solution $y = f(x)$ to the differential equation with the initial condition $f(0) = 4$. Write your answer in explicit form.

**(c)** For the particular solution found in part (b), find the domain of $f$.

**(d)** Let $g$ be another solution to the differential equation with initial condition $g(0) = -2$. Write the particular solution $y = g(x)$ in explicit form and determine whether $g$ is increasing or decreasing at $x = 1$. Justify your answer.

**4.** Let $f$ be the function defined by $f(x) = \frac{x}{1 + x^2}$.

**(a)** Find the first four nonzero terms and the general term of the Maclaurin series for $f(x)$. State the interval of convergence.

**(b)** Use the Maclaurin series found in part (a) to write the first four nonzero terms of the Maclaurin series for $\displaystyle g(x) = \int_0^x \frac{t}{1 + t^2}\,dt$. Express $g(x)$ in closed form and verify your series is consistent with the known Maclaurin expansion of that closed form.

**(c)** Write the first four nonzero terms of the Maclaurin series for $h(x) = \frac{x}{(1 + x^2)^2}$. (Hint: differentiate the series from part (a) or manipulate it.)

**(d)** Use the series from part (a) to approximate $\displaystyle \int_0^{1/2} \frac{x}{1 + x^2}\,dx$ with an error less than $\frac{1}{1000}$. Justify your error bound.

---

**5.** Let $R$ be the region in the first quadrant bounded by the curves $y = \sqrt{x}$, $y = 0$, and $x = 4$.

**(a)** Find the area of region $R$.

**(b)** Find the volume of the solid generated when $R$ is revolved about the $x$-axis.

**(c)** Find the volume of the solid generated when $R$ is revolved about the line $y = 3$.

**(d)** Region $R$ is the base of a solid. For this solid, at each $x$ the cross section perpendicular to the $x$-axis is a square whose side lies in $R$. Find the volume of this solid.

---

**6.** A particle moves in the $xy$-plane so that its position at time $t$, for $0 \le t \le 3$, is given by the parametric equations

$$x(t) = 2t - t^2, \qquad y(t) = t^3 - 3t$$

**(a)** Find the velocity vector and the speed of the particle at $t = 1$.

**(b)** Find $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ in terms of $t$. For what values of $t$ in $(0, 3)$ is the tangent line to the curve horizontal? Vertical?

**(c)** Find $\dfrac{d^2y}{dx^2}$ at $t = 1$. Is the curve concave up or concave down at this point?

**(d)** Find the total distance traveled by the particle from $t = 0$ to $t = 2$.
